Exploring The Prosper Loans Dataset¶

by Patrick Maloney¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this dataset, I wanted to find out more about the effects certain factors of a loan could have on whether or not the loans would be paid back on time or delinquent, and how investors responded to certain factors.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consisted of over 110,000 peer-to-peer loans made on the lending site Prosper, and contained over 80 different variables. I chose to focus on about 10 of these variables, so I did some wrangling around the variables I chose, filtering out those that did not have information in the places I was looking at. I also removed 39 outliers who reported making over 50k per month, which was skewing the data. I ended up with data on about 77k loans for my analysis, and I also ended up lumping different stages of compliance and delinquency into two bins: Compliant and Delinquent.

Distribution of Original Loan Amounts¶

The loans ranged from $1,000 - $35,000, and appear trimodal when plotted on a logarithmic scale, with peaks at 4-5k, 10k, and 15k.

Distribution of Number of Investors¶

Most Investors are embracing tthe spirit of peer-to-peer lending and choosing to independently fund nearly a third of all loans. When plotted logarithimcally, we see things still skew to fewer investors, but there is a more normal distribution between 30 and 200 investors.

Status Bin vs. Prosper Score¶

The loans in the delinquent bin skewed lower on the Prosper score scale than those in the compliant bin, and there was a much higher frequency of 8s and above in the compliant bin than the delinquent one, while it looks like 5s and 6s are the most common scores in the delinquent bin. Interestingly, there is a higher frequency of 3s in the compliant bin than the delinquent bin.

Status Bin vs. Investors¶

When plotted logarithmically, we can see that delinquent loans were much more likely to have more investors than compliant loans. Matched by width to show the discrepancy in the distribution, we see the bulk of the Compliance bin is around the 1 mark, whereas the bulk of delinquents are around the 20-40 marks. The internal boxplots show the discrepancy and higher median for the delinquent bin.

Investors by Prosper Score and Status Bin¶

In this swarmplot, we see that the number of investors increases as Prosper Score increases, while delinquencies are most dense in the middle ratings, and there are very few orange dots on the bottom row that signifies single investors.
